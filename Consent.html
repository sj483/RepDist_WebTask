Invitation to take part

You are invited to take part in a research study, which aims to further our understanding of why we can learn some pieces of information more quickly than others. Please read the following information sheet carefully. You can save a copy of this page for your records by clicking on this link.

The study is being conducted by student researcher Sophie Johnson (sj483@sussex.ac.uk), and project supervisor Dr Sam Berens (s.berens@sussex.ac.uk). They are based within the School of Psychology at the University of Sussex (UK) and are happy to be contacted if you have any questions.

Why have I been invited to take part and what will I do?

We are testing people between the ages of 18 and 45. You have been selected to take part because your user profile on Prolific.co/SONA indicates that you meet the inclusion criteria.

The experiment will involve a web-based trial-and-error task, which will take approximately 20 minutes to complete.

  • On each trial, you will see two images presented side-by-side.
  • You will then have 4 seconds to choose which of the images in this pair 'wins'. Once you have selected an answer you will receive feedback indicating whether you were correct.
  • The winning image in each pair will be determined by a simple set of rules that you will have to figure out using trial-and-error .

Are there any risks or benefits to taking part?

We anticipate being able to provide a summary of our findings on request from 1st September 2026. Please contact Sophie Johnson (sj483@sussex.ac.uk) if you wish to receive a copy of this report.
